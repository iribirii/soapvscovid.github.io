Stage 3:

How does soap work?

Before telling you why soap is so important in our fight against coronavirus (and viruses in general), let me first introduce a friend to you. His name is sodium stearate and he is the most common soap. He has hundres of close cousins that we use as soaps, but we will focus on him for this explanation.

Out little friend has two different parts that make him such a wonderful molecule for cleaning. His head, wich is formed by a carboxilate group and a sodium anion. And his body formed by an aliphatic chain. Both can be seen in the image below.

But why is this important? Well, it is actually the key of everything. The head LOVES water and it is very attracted to it. On the other hand, the body HATES water, so it tray to be as far as it can from it. Due to this bipolar nature, soap molecules always try to positions themselves in a very special way where all the heads are close to each other and so are the bodies between them. This special we of organizing in the space, called micelle, is very similar to the way that our cells membranes are created. And also to the way that viruses are created.

This similarity between the membrane in viruses and soap is the main reason why soap can disolve them. It is acting like a bridge between the particles forming the membrane and the surrounding water, favouring the interaction between them and the solvation of the virus. Once this interaction happens many soap molecules start to introduce their bodies deeper in the virus membrane and destabilizing it. Eventually, some parts of the membrane will separate from the main body and will be surrounded by soap molecules, encapsulating them. This will be like a chain reaction in which the more particles are separated, the easier and faster the rest will be disolved too. And once the membrane is desintegrated and the DNA released in the water, the virus is completly destroyed and the result is a lot of different virus building blocks encapsulated in soap micelles floating all over the solution.

As you can imagine this process is not as simple as explained here, the virus membrane is not only build by one type of molecule and different virus will have different structures and types of molecules. But the idea you have to take from here is that soap is very important for preventing the spread of COVID-19. And now you know why.
